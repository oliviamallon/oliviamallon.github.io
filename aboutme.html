About Me

I'm Olivia Mallon, I'm 18 and I'm studying Computer Science MEng at Queen's University Belfast.
I enjoy trying new things - so for my 18th Birthday I went skydiving with my mum and brother. (I couldn't recommend it enough!)
I enjoy reading, walking/hiking, calligraphy and figuring out problems - computer/non-computer related.

This photo was taken when I went skydiving in Septemebr 2020. After a long awaited birthday present we jumped out of a plane. Crazy? Some might say so but for me it taught me so much. I learnt that when sitting on edge of a plane with the door open, it takes a lot of trust. In the next second, I begin falling. I lose all sensation and just enjoy it. It doesn't feel real in the slightest. Yet, I begin looking and see the incredible world that we live in. As people we tend to think the world centres around us (whether you want to admit it or not). But in that second you see just how small everything is, from 10,000ft above ground. After that the parachute goes and you can enjoy the view, thinking - did that really just happen? That's what I believe trusting God is like. An exciting adventure, we don't know what is going to happen but we can trust seeing the beauty of his creation around us.

I really enjoy helping people. I volunteer on different camps and teams every summer and have always come away with so many memories. The biggest take away from the all is definitely that my heart grew to love people more than ever. My belief is if we truly care for the world we live in, we'll do something about it. Technology is a weird and wonderful thing. I love the example of google Translate. It was first made to bring two people together who couldn't speak the others language. Yet technology has advanced so much, that people will sit in the same room and pay more attention to an endless timeline on Instagram/Facebook rather than the real connections they can have with people in person. Technology can be used for good or bad, but it all starts with the design, and that's where I want to help.
